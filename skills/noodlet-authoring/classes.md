# Classes, students, and progress

## Create a class

Teacher app → **Classes** → **New class**. Each class gets a reusable open invite
link — share it by email, chat, or QR code. Students who follow it join immediately:
logged-in accounts enrol under their account; others join anonymously (device-bound,
progress is lost if they clear cookies or switch devices unless they claim an account).

Rotate the link in class settings if it leaks — enrolled students are unaffected.

## Enrol students

Three ways:

1. **Invite link (self-service)** — share the class URL; students join on their own.
   Best for classes where students already have (or will create) their own accounts.
2. **Add student** — for students who don't have a Noodlet account yet. Provide a
   username (globally unique across the platform) and optional display name; an initial
   password is shown for the teacher to share with the student.
3. **Invite by username** — for students who already have an account. They see an
   accept/reject prompt the next time they log in.

Anonymous students can register or log in at any time; Noodlet automatically claims
their anonymous progress onto the account — no teacher action needed. If they already
had an account-bound enrolment in the same class the two merge, uniting all attempts
under a single student identity.

## Assign a noodlet

Class → **Assignments** → add the noodlet. Students see it in their lesson list
immediately. Assignments survive re-publishing — the noodlet's URL stays stable when
you update and re-publish.

A noodlet can be assigned to multiple classes.

## Track progress

Class → **Progress** — per-student: attempt count, best score (0–1 mastery fraction),
and whether the student has completed the lesson.
